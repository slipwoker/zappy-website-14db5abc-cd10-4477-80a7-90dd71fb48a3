A solo-dev approach that stays human.

Every project begins with a simple question: what would feel right here? I prototype quickly, iterate on the core feel, then layer in visuals and audio that match the intended emotion. It's a personal loop—sketch, test, refine—until the experience clicks.

The goal isn't perfection on the first pass. It's finding the heart of an idea, then building around it. Mechanics come first, visuals follow, and polish happens when the foundation is solid. That's how an indie game developer keeps momentum without losing the thread.
